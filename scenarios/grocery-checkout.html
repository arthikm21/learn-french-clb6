🛒 Shopping · CLB 3 · ~7 min

At the grocery checkout

Bag-or-no-bag, debit-or-credit, loyalty card

The situation

You: You're paying for your groceries at Metro.

Them: The cashier is friendly but moves quickly.

Goal: Understand the small-talk + transaction questions, respond appropriately.

The conversation (French & English)

Bonjour ! Vous avez besoin d'un sac ?
Hello! Do you need a bag?

Oui, s'il vous plaît.
Yes, please.

Vous avez votre carte Metro&moi ?
Do you have your Metro&moi loyalty card?

Non, je n'en ai pas.
No, I don't have one.

D'accord. Ça fait quarante-sept dollars et cinquante.
OK. That comes to $47.50.

Je paie par carte.
I'll pay by card.

Débit ou crédit ?
Debit or credit?

Débit, s'il vous plaît.
Debit, please.

Voilà votre reçu. Bonne journée !
Here's your receipt. Have a good day!

Merci, à vous aussi !
Thanks, you too!

Grammar in this conversation — "en" — the small word for "any" / "some"

When someone asks if you have something and you don't — drop en in front of the verb. "Je n'en ai pas" = "I don't have any (of those)." Saves you from repeating the noun.

  • Vous avez une carte ? — Non, je n'en ai pas.
  • Tu veux du pain ? — Oui, j'en veux.
  • Il a des enfants ? — Il en a trois.

Phrases to practise aloud

Oui, s'il vous plaît. — Yes, please.

Non, je n'en ai pas. — No, I don't have one.

Answer: Debit

4. How much was the total?
Answer: $47.50

Your speaking challenge

Run through a checkout interaction. Cashier will ask: bag? loyalty card? debit or credit? Respond naturally to all three.
